A large, cylindrical water tank with diameter 3.00 m is on a platform 2.00 m above the ground. The vertical tank is open to the air and the depth of the water in the tank is 2.00 m. There is a hole with diameter 0.500 cm in the side of the tank just above the bottom of the tank. The hole is plugged with a cork. You remove the cork and collect in a bucket the water that flows out the hole. (a) When 1.00 gal of water flows out of the tank, what is the change in the height of the water in the tank? (b) How long does it take you to collect 1.00 gal of water in the bucket? Based on your answer in part (a), is it reasonable to ignore the change in the depth of the water in the tank as 1.00 gal of water flows out?
